Hop Bush by Agrim 9T

Overview

The Hop Bush is a shrub which varies from 1.5-4 metres in shrub height. It produces colourful fruits and has sticky leathery leaves. The Wiradjuri people of New South Wales use the traditional name of Bururr.

Where it grows
The hop bush grows in tropical and subtropical environments and is mainly found across central Australia but also grows in Western Australia, Queensland and New South Wales. It naturally occurs in arid regions, particularly in rocky areas.

Nutritional value identified by First Nations People
Most of the Nutritional value in modern day that's derived from the hop bush comes in the form of essential oil. There are anti-inflammatory particles in the hop bush so it can help lower your blood pressure and reduce the risk of a heart attack or cardiac issues.It also contains phenolics and phenolic compounds can inhibit the enzymes connected to the development of human diseases. Hop bush was also traditionally used by Aboriginal Australians to treat toothache, cuts and stingray stings because of the properties found within the fruit. For a toothache, they used hop bush as a bush medicine by chewing on the leaves of the hop bush. To treat jellyfish and other stings, the Aboriginal binded wads of chewed leaf pulp for a few days on the affected area. Because they did this, it acted as a natural painkiller.

How we use (your fruit) today
Hop bush can be used to treat toothaches so it improves your dental health. It is also known to have properties that can treat stingray stings, so the sting can be less painful and cured. Furthermore, Hop bush excelles in treating cuts because they reduce inflammation and swelling.

Why we should farm (your fruit)
Hop bush grows best in arid regions and 70% of Australia is either arif or semi arid land so you can grow it optimally anywhere. Hop bush can be found across all states and territories but it is more commonly found in Central Australia, followed by Western Australia, Queensland and New South Wales.The plant itself does best in soil with good drainage. It has an upright growth habit that requires plenty of sunlight to reach its full height so you can get many leaves and fruit out of the plant. Hop bush is simple to maintain and grows quickly. Because it is simple to maintain and grows quickly, farmers will be able to harvest it and make more money easily. Additionally, the hop bush is hardy and long lived, lasting up to fifty years so it is an efficient source of money and contains many health benefits.
