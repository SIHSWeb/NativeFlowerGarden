Overview

Lomandra Hystrix (or Mat Rush) is a fast-growing compact plant with fine bright green foliage and yellow spiny flower spikes during spring. Lomandra Hysterixâ€™s yellow flowers are pleasantly perfumed with a sweet, unusual smell.

Where it grows
Lomandra Hystrix can be grown throughout eastern Australia. It is most commonly grown near the coast, creeks, and boggy areas. Lomandra Hysterix can be grown in a wide range of soil from light (sandy) to heavy (clay) soil, as there is no special pH level, or soil it should be grown in because its long thin-spreading roots make it easy to grow anywhere. Lomandra Hystrix can be grown anywhere from minimal amounts to high amounts of sun. It requires low-to-minimal maintenance and attention.

Nutritional value identified by First Nations People
The Indigenous Australians commonly used Lomandra Hystrix for weaving baskets because the leaves of the plant are sturdy, bendy and thin enough to be used to weave with. However, there are many other uses for Lomandra Hystrix. One of the most common uses is as a bushtucker. Surprisingly, pulling the leaves in the centre of the plant out carefully reveals a white fleshy part at the bottom of the leaves. First Nations people would wash the clumps in the nearby stream and then chew the white base. It tastes like sweet peas and has a crunchy texture. The leaves are full of fibre and various nutrients. As well as the leaves, Indigenous Australians would eat the white-grey kernels inside the seeds because the seeds are full of carbohydrates and are very nutritious.

How we use (your fruit) today
Lomandra Hystrix is still used around Australia for many reasons. One of the main uses is as a low-lying, landscaping plant. It is also used to hold soil in place because of the long roots. But, there are many other uses for it, it could be used as a gluten-free substitute for flour. The seeds could be used as a substitute for nuts due to the high levels of carbohydrates and proteins.

Australia should farm Lomandra as an easily available substitute.
