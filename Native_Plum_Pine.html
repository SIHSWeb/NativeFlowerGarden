Native Plum Pine by Hayden 9T

Overview

The plum pine or the native plum is a indigenous round plant that shares many of its characteristics with a normal plum. The plum pine is known by the indigenous name Goongum.

Where it grows
The plum pine grows in both Eastern New South Wales and Eastern Queensland, in temperate regions. With reference to the soil it grows on, the plant is observed to grow the best on good rich soil, and with plenty of water.

Nutritional value identified by First Nations People
The first nations people found many nutritional benefits with the plum pine. The most notable benefit is its massive vitamin C content, the plum pine is so rich in vitamin C it is a contender for the title of the highest amount of vitamin C in a fruit per 100 grams. The plum pine has a reported vitamin C content of 3150 mg per 100 g (fresh weight). It also is traditionally used to fight colds, coughs, infections, and headaches because of high antioxidant values, containing more than 3 times the value of a blueberry.. Allegedly, the plum pine may play a role in preventing cancer because of the high content of anthocyanin-rich phenolics.

Why we should farm (your fruit)
Australian farmers should consider growing the Illawarra Plum Pine because it's a hardy native species well-suited to local conditions. The Illawarra Plum Pine is drought tolerant and can thrive in various soil types, making it a low-maintenance crop. But its greatest potential lies in its unique fruit. Unlike many other fruit trees, the Illawarra Plum Pine produces edible fruit with a distinctive piney flavour. This unique taste can appeal to both domestic and international markets, so it could be a profitable addition to Australian agriculture.
